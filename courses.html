A two day fully recognized certificated course. Fully accredited by the Federation of Prenatal Education (FEDANT) and Independent Professional Therapists International (IPTI) This course covers the first 1001 critical days, birth to standing.
Peter Walker, who has devoted the past forty years of his professional life to supporting mothers and babies. This programme seeks to alleviate birth trauma and limit the effects of physical problems like stiff and floppy baby syndromes associated with birth and inter-uterine trauma and conditions like Prader Willi, Cerebral Palsy and others (unknown).
A copy of Peter Walker’s international best selling book ‘Developmental Baby Massage’ for all delegates. Plus all course notes for delegates and session notes for parents Bi-monthly news letter Post course support given for all teachers.
Lori Boggan, a Registered Neo Natal Nurse,“Having worked many years in the NeoNatal Intensive Care Unit, it is easy to wonder what the outcomes of some of the extremely premature and asphyxiated babies are – those that leave with the diagnosis of developmental delay. Watching one of Peter’s documentaries during our training gave me a sense hope.
Key Teachings in DBM Course

1) Emotional Intelligence Loving Touch / Bonding and Attachment: Developing a loving touch, secure holding, and safe tummy time,Remedial Touch techniques to relieve intra-uterine and difficult birth experience, Holding and stroking techniques prior to massage to relieve physiological flexion, Techniques to release the phrenic nerve.

2) Developmental Baby Massage: Massage Techniques and strokes are demonstrated to parents using a doll, with emphasis on loving touch. Infant Development Nature’s Milestones Joint Flexibility, Muscular Strength and Coordination. Improving circulatory, respiratory rhythms, back strength and overall muscle tone.

3) Infant Balance and Good Posture First Sitting Position to Second Sitting position. Second Sitting Position to Third Sitting Position Third Sitting / Squatting Crawling and Standing Standing and Walking Primary preparation for sitting and standing using gravity assisted techniques for stronger structural foundations

4) Therapeutic Play to Relieve Stiff and Floppy Baby Syndromes: Early Intervention and Enablement Motivation and Movement Cranial Neuroplasticity Gravity and Centralised Movement. Remedial Touch Massage and Movement to relieve stiffness Floppy Babies Gaining and Regaining Head Control, Subdueing the Moro Reflex Flexibility Strength and Coordination Gaining / Re gaining Arm and Hand Control Building Back and Core Strength.

5) Contra-Indications, Safety, correct baby massage oils and usage.

6) Convening and Teaching one to one and groups
